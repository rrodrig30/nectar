# NECTAR Software Design Document

**The clinician-facing application for the NECTAR platform**

Version 3.0 · Author: Ronald Rodriguez, MD, PhD · UT Health San Antonio
Status: Draft for review

> NECTAR reads the shared database and composes recommendations per patient. It never writes the shared graph except through the single gated verification path. Schema is defined in [`../../contract/DATA_CONTRACT.md`](../../contract/DATA_CONTRACT.md). Implementation is in [`PDD.md`](./PDD.md).

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

NECTAR sits in front of a primary care physician managing a complex, multimorbid patient. It converts abstracted clinical data into a resolved dietary constraint set, identifies target recipes and preparation methods that meet the patient's conditions, medications, and goals, and presents approachable recipes and meal plans with calculated nutrition. It also runs a research channel for proposing and later verifying preparation techniques.

**In scope**: the patient abstraction layer, the recommendation engine, meal planning, presentation and normalization, the interactive question-and-answer and modification surface, and the research and verification channel.

**Out of scope**: building the database (NutriScrape), any diagnostic function, and any patient-facing deployment without the clinical validation gate in Section 9.

---

## 2. Intended User and Boundary

The user is a clinician or researcher, not a patient. The physician enters de-identified abstracted data and reviews recommendations. Every recommendation is an educational and research output, not medical nutrition therapy, and carries that boundary. The patient abstraction crosses into clinical inference, so the clinician confirms every derived constraint before it drives a recommendation (Section 3.3).

---

## 3. The Patient Abstraction Layer

This is the clinically consequential part of NECTAR and the piece a reviewer will scrutinize hardest. It turns a clinical snapshot into the resolved constraint set the engine evaluates.

### 3.1 Inputs
Past medical history, a recent metabolic panel, a recent CBC, active medications, allergies, objective data (age, sex, weight, height, activity level), and the nutritional goal (weight loss, weight gain, weight maintenance with increased muscle mass, cardiovascular improvement, and so on).

### 3.2 Derivation
- Conditions become disease `:DietaryRule` sets.
- Medications become interaction rules against the compound and attribute vocabularies.
- Allergies become Stage 1 hard filters.
- Labs derive physiologic state and numeric targets. Serum potassium and phosphorus tighten or relax renal ceilings; creatinine with age and sex gives eGFR and CKD stage; albumin informs protein; glucose informs glycemic load; a low absolute neutrophil count switches on the raw-food safety exclusion; hemoglobin flags iron need and pulls in tannin and phytate bioavailability rules.
- Objective data and goal set the energy and protein envelope through validated equations.

### 3.3 Deterministic derivation, physician confirmation [INVARIANT]
The derivation of state and numeric targets is deterministic from validated formulas and the guideline knowledge base, not produced by a language model. A model may parse free-text history into structured factors, but the factor-to-number step is code. Every derived constraint is shown to the physician for confirmation or override before it drives a single recommendation. The system proposes the abstraction; the clinician owns it.

---

## 4. Recommendation Engine

Over the confirmed constraint set, NECTAR runs the two-stage evaluation against the contract's recipe-variant facts.

Stage 1 is a hard filter on absolute rules: allergens, absolute drug-food interactions, and food-safety exclusions gated by physiologic state. Stage 2 is a graded multi-objective score over survivors, with directional conflicts resolved by precedence rather than averaging. Safety dominates goals, so a weight-loss goal never relaxes a renal potassium ceiling.

Ranking happens at two levels. Across dishes, best admissible version per dish. Within a dish, the real corpus versions ranked for this patient, which is the clinically useful mode because it meets the patient at a food they already want. When the corpus holds no admissible version, and only then, the remediation engine proposes a preparation change to the closest one, generated by matching the failing target's mechanism to its intervention classes, and labeled as a suggested modification rather than an existing recipe. Every remediation reruns the full constraint set against the modified recipe, so a change that fixes one target and breaks another is rejected or flagged.

Tier C hypotheses never enter this path. They belong to the research channel (Section 8).

---

## 5. Meal Planning

The weekly meal plan is a constrained selection across dishes, and it is where plan-level `maintain` rules finally land. Warfarin vitamin K consistency, daily fluid ceilings, and daily energy and protein envelopes cannot be evaluated against a single recipe, so they are evaluated across the plan window. The planner balances variety, the daily envelopes, and the plan-level consistency constraints while drawing only from admissible dishes.

---

## 6. Presentation and Normalization

The database holds canonical units. NECTAR converts at the edge.

- Unit system (US or metric) and temperature scale (Fahrenheit or Celsius) are user-selectable and applied at render time from the canonical values. One stored truth, a display transform, never two stored copies.
- Serving sizes are standardized for the patient and mapped from the canonical per-serving basis.
- Every nutrient value shows the calculated-not-measured disclaimer, made specific by the contract's confidence and source on that value.
- Recipes are surfaced at a reading level and complexity approachable to the patient.

---

## 7. Interaction

A question-and-answer surface parses the clinician's natural-language requests into structured queries and narrates the engine's rankings in grounded prose with citations. These are the two language-model touchpoints in the query path, at the ends, never in the scoring middle.

Recipe modification for taste or for available equipment runs as a constraint. No grill, no convection oven, no stand mixer becomes an equipment exclusion that reruns the same evaluation and re-ranks, because a method the patient cannot perform is another filter. Taste modifications are handled the same way, as added preferences or exclusions.

Backend settings are NECTAR runtime configuration: model choice across Ollama, Anthropic, or OpenAI, hyperparameters, and context window. These are exposed to the operator because NECTAR runs the interactive language work. They are distinct from NutriScrape's batch extraction models.

---

## 8. Research and Verification Channel

The research option surfaces Tier C hypotheses for a target, generates an experimental-design stub (mechanism, endpoint, assay, baseline to beat, confounders to control), and accepts real measured preparation data.

That measured data is the platform's one write-back. Through the contract's gated promotion path, a submission with a linked measurement record and a named reviewer promotes a transform or hypothesis from proposed toward verified. This is the only mutation NECTAR performs on the shared graph, and it is what moves a technique from research-only to patient-eligible over time.

---

## 9. Clinical Safety, Governance, and Intended Use

> NECTAR is educational and research software. Its output is not medical nutrition therapy and is not validated for individual patient care. It must not direct a real patient's diet outside a supervised research protocol with appropriate oversight.

Safety is architecture, not a footer. The deterministic derivation with physician confirmation, the Stage 1 hard filter, the safety-dominant conflict rule, the Tier C research quarantine, and confidence propagation exist so the system fails toward caution. Before any patient contact, the validation gate applies: dietitian and physician review of constraints, retrospective agreement studies per condition, prospective evaluation under protocol, and IRB approval for any patient data or patient-facing output.

A system that recommends dietary management for named diseases can meet the definition of clinical decision support software. As a research and educational tool making no diagnostic or treatment claims, NECTAR stays outside device claims; a move toward patient-facing use requires a deliberate regulatory assessment.

---

## 10. Security and Privacy

No patient records are persisted. The abstracted profile is de-identified and transient, used to compose a recommendation and not stored with identity. Access is authenticated and role-scoped (researcher, clinical reviewer, administrator). The local generation option (Ollama) keeps sensitive input on institutional infrastructure. The only persistent write is the audited verification record in the shared graph, which contains no patient data.

---

## 11. Non-Functional Requirements

| Attribute | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Latency | Interactive: recommendation served from precomputed contract facts plus query-time composition |
| Reproducibility | A recommendation traces to the confirmed constraints, the contract facts used, and the contract version |
| Extensibility | New conditions and drugs enter through the knowledge base, not code |
| Auditability | Derived constraints and rankings are explainable to the governing rule and guideline |
| Contract fidelity | Reads pin the contract version; write-back conforms to the gated path |
